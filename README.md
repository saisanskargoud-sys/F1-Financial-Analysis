UK F1 Teams Financial Analysis (2021-2023)

Equity research analyzing financial performance and franchise valuation of UK-based Formula 1 teams.
Project Overview

Independent financial analysis of 4 UK F1 teams (McLaren, Williams, Mercedes, Aston Martin) using Companies House filings (2021-2023).
Methodology
Financial Analysis:
- Extracted 3 years of audited financial data from Companies House
- Calculated 10+ ratios: profitability, efficiency, leverage
- Comparative analysis across all teams
Valuation:
- Revenue Multiple (6x industry benchmark)
- EBITDA Multiple (15x sports franchise standard)
- DCF model with 5-year projections
Tools:
- Excel (financial modeling, ratio analysis)
- Tableau (interactive dashboard)
- Bloomberg Terminal (market data)
Key Findings
Revenue & Scale:
- Mercedes leads: £546M revenue, £3.3B estimated value
- McLaren: 102% revenue growth (2021-2023)
- Williams: Smallest by revenue (£127M)
Profitability:
- Williams achieved exceptional 66% EBIT margin (2023)
- Industry average: 8-10% margins
- Aston Martin: Declining from 28% to 9%
Valuation Insights:
- Revenue-based valuations significantly exceed profitability-based
- Williams shows inverse relationship: low revenue, high EBITDA valuation
- DCF suggests £6.8B for McLaren vs £2.6B revenue multiple
